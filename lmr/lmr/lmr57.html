Machines cannot read because they do not have access to the substance, the voice, of language. They will not be able to read documentary, encoded literature as language until and unless they can. And they will not do so as long as a systematically stupid computational, structuralist model of language and mind prevails. “Distant reading” is not reading in any constitutive or aesthetically tractable sense of the term. Typically, it produces statements for the sociology of literature, or decorative, literary, critical-aesthetic, suggestively prescriptive sociology.
