The language that machines “read” – at any particular moment – has been excerpted or extracted from what Walter Ong calls a grapholect, “a transdialect language formed by deep commitment to writing” (Ong 1982, 8). A little more precisely, machines read encoded transcriptions of grapholects that are tightly integrated with natural languages. The plural is important. We should not constrain our attention to the absurdly predominant global English grapholect.
