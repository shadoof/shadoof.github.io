“Generative” is reduced to the combinatorial in Chomsky’s “generative grammar,” the predominant paradigmatic model of language as computable, amongst contemporary scientistic approaches. The structural linguistics of generative grammar is one of the major symptoms of an underlying conviction – generally widespread, but pervasive in the scientific community – that mind and thought are reducible to physical processes which can be formulated. The ascendancy of the regime of computation since the Second World War has provided a plausible mechanism for fundamentally misdirected theories and “simulations” of language as designative.
