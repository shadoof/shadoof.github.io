All traces of language indicate an archi-writing in Jacques Derrida’s sense. This relation is fundamentally synaesthetic, since our experience encompasses all that we perceive, and in language we trace our experience. The processes of grammalepsis are support media agnostic. Any perceptible and malleable medium will do, in principle. After all, these processes – (grammaleptic) reading by another name – render gestures that are expressed in any perceptible and malleable medium as ontologically and diegetically distinct, broken out and away from their support medium, and thus suddenly and simultaneously in the world of language. This is not to say, however, that any artificially or aesthetically structured substance or artifact or performance in structured artifactuality can or should be treated as or like (a) language, because not all such structures are readable.
